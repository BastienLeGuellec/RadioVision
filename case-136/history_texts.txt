A 57-year-old man presented with a 3-month history of abdominal pain. Physical examination revealed a large firm palpable mass in the epigastric region. There was no history of alcoholism, trauma, or previous pancreatitis. Laboratory findings were unremarkable. The patient underwent abdominal upright radiography, upper gastrointestinal barium examination, abdominal ultrasonography (US) (,Fig 1), and abdominal computed tomography (CT) (,Fig 2,,,).
